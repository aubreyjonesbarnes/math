Some expressions of the form ax2 + bx + c are special because they can be solved in a unique way.
Some polynomials have a special pattern.  Recog-nition of this pattern makes  them easier to factor. This lesson shows two of these special cases: perfect trinomial squares and the difference of two squares.
RULE 1
Trinomials of the type a2 + 2ab + b2 or a2 - 2ab + b2 are called perfect trinomial squares.  Factoring a perfect trinomial square gives the square of a binomial.
a2 + 2ab + b2 = (a + b)2 a2 - 2ab + b2 = (a - b)2
