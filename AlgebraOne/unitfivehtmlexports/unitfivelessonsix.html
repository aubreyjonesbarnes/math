You correctly factored (r - 3) as the common factor, but it should not be squared. It does appear twice in the original problem, but that is what makes it a common factor. You should simply multiply (r - 3) by the remaining factors.
Remember, you compare each term to see if there are any common factors, or any factors that are the same.   For example, the first term has a 5, but the second term does not have a 5, so 5 is not a factor.
3 and r are both found in each term but they are found within parentheses joined by a minus sign and must remain in that form.  The entire expression, (r - 3) can be factored out.

After factorization by grouping, be sure to check each resulting factor for any possible further factorization.

= (4a2m + 4a2) + (-25m - 25)
