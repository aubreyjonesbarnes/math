A Balanced Equation
This balance represents an equation.
An equation has an equal sign with two equal sides. 
Equations
An equation is a mathematical sentence made up of two expressions connected by the “equals” symbol, “=.”
These are examples of equations.
4 + 5(a + 6) = (a - 7)9 - a
6 = 2 + 4
2a + 5 = 7(a - 5)
Adding a number to both sides of an equation transforms it into an equivalent equation.
2a = 12
Equivalent Equations
3 + 2a = 12 + 3
Equivalent equations are equations that have the same solution. 
This is true because the Addition Property for Equations states, for all numbers a, b, and c, 
if a = b, then a + c = b + c. 
2a - 3 = 12 + (-3)
Subtracting a number from both sides of an equation also transforms it into an equivalent equation, since subtracting a number is the same as adding its opposite.

“Undoing” an Operation
NOTE
In science, many operations undo one another. For example, melting “undoes” freezing. Likewise, freezing “undoes” melting.
Inverse Operations
Addition and subtraction are inverse operations. This means that one operation “undoes” the other  and your answer/equation doesn't change.
