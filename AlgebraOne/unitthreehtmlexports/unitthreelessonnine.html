A Lever Called a Balance  A balance works like a lever. Suppose a balance has a lever that is 1 foot long, with a fulcrum exactly in the center. Somebody puts 5 pounds of sugar on one  side of the balance. How many pounds of sugar must be added to the other side to balance it?
Both sides must have 5 pounds of sugar to be equal.
Answer: When the fulcrum is exactly in the center, the weight on both sides must be the same in order to balance. In this problem the lever extends one-half foot—the same distance on either side.
