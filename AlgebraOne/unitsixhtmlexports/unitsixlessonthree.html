In Algebra, it is also not practical to work with polynomials written in this form:
-5 + x2 - 2x

We try to put polynomials in practical form, or descending order, so that they will be easier to use.
A polynomial is in practical form when written in descending order of exponents with the first, or leading, coefficient represented as a positive integer.
RULE 1
It is sometimes necessary to factor -1 from a polynomial to obtain a positive leading coefficient.
