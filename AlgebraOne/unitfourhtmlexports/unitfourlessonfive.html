Julie is a clerk for the art department.
One of her jobs is to distribute supplies to the three art teachers.
One morning, Julie had to pass out red pens.  There were seven in the box.
Julie determined that she could not pass out 7 pens to the 3 art teachers and have each of them get the same amount.
1(7) = 7

When she tried to find any way to divide seven evenly, she only found one way:
The next day, Julie had to distribute a box of charcoal pencils.
The box had 36 pencils.
In order for each teacher to get the same amount of pencils, 3 must divide into 36 evenly.
3 and 12 are factors of 36  because they divide 36 evenly.
36 ÷ 3 = 12 In this case, each teacher would get 12 pencils.
We can continue to find more factors of 36 until we have found all the prime factors.
